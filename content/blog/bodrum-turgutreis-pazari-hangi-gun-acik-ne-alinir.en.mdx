## The Ritual of Saturday Morning

Along the Turgutreis shore, every Saturday morning, the broadest and most vibrant market on the Bodrum peninsula is set up. On the wide road running parallel to the sea, the stalls begin to line up from the sixth hour of morning, and a shopping ritual that lasts until noon begins. In the spring months, in the early morning hours when the coolness is still fresh, a trip to the market becomes an experience in which both the local people and the guests staying in the area touch upon everyday life. The broad, orderly avenues of Turgutreis become, on market days, the stage for a riot of colour, a tangle of scents and friendly bargaining conversations. This market is, far more than an ordinary shopping spot, a social gathering place that offers a cross-section of Bodrum culture. To visit the Turgutreis market on Saturday mornings is the most intimate way to feel the everyday rhythm of the peninsula.

## Fresh Produce and Local Flavours

The most striking part of the market is the fresh fruit and vegetable stalls. In spring the stalls hold an abundance of fresh broad beans, artichokes, spinach, rocket and seasonal greens. Local growers carry the produce they pick in the early morning hours straight to their stalls, and this freshness is immediately apparent both to the eye and to the taste. Seasonal fruits such as oranges, mandarins and strawberries are a feast for the eyes with their bright colours and intense aromas. In the inner parts of the market, products particular to Bodrum, such as olive oil, dried figs, almonds, walnuts and sun-dried tomatoes, are sold. Cold-pressed olive oil and fresh brined olives in particular are products that reflect the agricultural richness of the area. Stopping at these stalls during a market trip is the most natural way to obtain the basic ingredients of Bodrum cuisine at their source.

## Seafood and Fish Stalls

The Turgutreis Market's proximity to the sea reinforces the presence of fresh fish and seafood stalls. In the early morning hours, sea bass, sea bream, red mullet, horse mackerel and squid, brought in from the sea, take their place on the iced stalls. In the spring months, when the sea temperature has only begun to rise, the flavour of the fish is at its peak. The conversations that form around the fish stalls turn into intimate moments where local knowledge about fish species, cooking methods and the state of the sea is shared. For guests staying in Turgutreis, preparing market fish in the villa kitchen is the most direct way to add a local taste to the holiday experience. Alongside the fish, fresh octopus, mussels and prawns can also be seen on the stalls according to the season. When buying seafood, lending an ear to the vendors' advice is of benefit both in choosing fresh produce and in cooking techniques.

## Crafts and Traditional Goods

Another important part of the market is the stalls displaying handcrafted goods and traditional Turkish crafts. Woven bags, woven throws, ceramic plates, copper jugs and wooden kitchenware offer choices that are both decorative and functional. In the spring months, in the period before the tourist season has intensified, more intimate and longer conversations can be struck up at these stalls. Handcrafted goods are like keepsakes that carry the cultural texture of Bodrum. In particular, the lace knitted by local women, embroidered kitchen towels and knitted baskets are products in which the knowledge of traditional craft has been carried into the present day. To spend time at these stalls during a market trip is a way of experiencing not only the natural beauty of Bodrum but also its cultural richness. The Turgutreis Market is, in this respect, not merely a shopping spot but also a living exhibition of local life and traditions.

## Clothing, Textiles and Everyday Goods

The broad area of the market also makes room for clothing and textile products. Cotton dresses, beachwear, hats, slippers and everyday accessories are displayed on the stalls at reasonable prices. In the spring season, light fabrics and pale colours come to the fore. Alongside local producers, wholesale textile products also occupy a wide space at the market. When shopping, bargaining is both a traditional and an economical method. Prices at the Turgutreis Market are generally flexible, and a courteous round of bargaining makes for an enjoyable exchange for both vendor and buyer. Alongside the clothing stalls, home textiles, bedspreads, towels and kitchen accessories are also found in abundance. This section is an area where guests on a villa stay can discover practical and aesthetic products.

## Making the Most of the Market Experience

The most ideal time to wander the Turgutreis Market is the slot between eight and twelve in the morning. At these hours the stalls are still fresh and the crowd is at a manageable level. In the spring months, the air temperature is also suited to strolling. Taking a cloth bag or basket with you makes carrying your purchases easier and is at the same time an environmentally friendly choice. As you wander the market, strolling among the stalls without haste, chatting with the vendors and listening to the stories of the products deepens the experience. The dialogues struck up with local producers in particular offer valuable opportunities to understand the agricultural and cultural texture of Bodrum. After a market trip, having a coffee at one of the cafés along the Turgutreis shore is a ritual that rounds off the pleasure of the morning. The Turgutreis Market is, for guests staying in Bodrum, both a practical shopping spot and the most intimate way to take the pulse of local life. Enriching your Saturday mornings with this market experience adds a different dimension to your holiday.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Which day and at what times is the Turgutreis Market open?**  
The Turgutreis Market begins to be set up every Saturday morning at around 06:00 and continues until noon. The busiest and freshest hours are between 08:00 and 12:00.

**What kinds of goods can be found at the market?**  
Fresh fruit and vegetables, seafood, olive oil, olives, spices, handcrafted goods, textiles, clothing, household items and local flavours can all be found. There is a broad range of products for both food and everyday needs.

**Is it possible to haggle?**  
Yes, a courteous round of bargaining is normal, especially at the clothing and textile stalls. With fruit, vegetables and food products, price reductions can also be made for larger quantities.

**Should one prefer cash or card?**  
Most stalls at the market accept cash only. Keeping small notes makes shopping easier. Some larger stalls may accept cards, but carrying cash is safer.

**Is it convenient to go to the market by car?**  
As the Turgutreis Market is set up along the shore, finding parking can be difficult. Going early or arriving on foot is more practical. There are paid car parks on the nearby streets.
