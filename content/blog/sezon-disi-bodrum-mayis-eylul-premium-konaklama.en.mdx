## Redefining the Off-Season

In Bodrum, the word "season" classically describes the period stretching from mid-June to early September. In recent years, however, a matured approach to boutique holidays calls for this pattern to be reconsidered. Late May into early June, and September into mid-October — these edges of the season are in fact Bodrum's most mature weeks. With quiet coves, balanced prices and a still-warm sea, the truest time for a boutique villa stay is frequently hidden here.

This guide treats these two periods, referred to as off-season, as a mature accommodation choice. Each of the May and September weeks has its own rhythm, atmosphere and advantages.

## May: The Opening of the Season

From mid-May onwards, Bodrum begins to receive guests as if awoken from its winter sleep. After the spring rains, the green of the plants is at its most saturated colour; laurel, rosemary and old olive trees shape the air of the slope like a perfume. The marina restaurants have just opened the season; the menus are carefully prepared, and the crowds have not yet formed.

In May the air temperature is generally around 22-26 degrees. This is an ideal temperature for spending long hours on the beach; the sun is not at a very steep angle, and the need for shade is slight. The sea temperature in mid-May is around 20-22 degrees; considered a little cool for active swimming, but short dips are comfortable. From late May the water temperature rises to 22-24 degrees and swimming becomes more comfortable.

In May the most distinctive advantage of a boutique villa stay is that a villa truly feels "private." The density of construction along the cove feels halved compared with the season's busy weeks; no sound comes from neighbouring properties, and there is little vehicle traffic on the coastal road. This is a decisive quality, especially for honeymoon couples.

## Early June: The Transition Weeks

The first ten days of June stand out as the mature weeks of the season. While May's calm is still felt, the sea temperature has risen to 23-25 degrees and all the season's restaurants have opened. Marina activity has begun but the density has not yet reached its peak. Beach club reservations are easy; the near-season (late May) prices apply.

After mid-June, pricing moves into the high-season band. This means that for guests who wish to experience the season in a mature week, early June becomes a particularly valuable time. Our concierge team points to the first ten days of June as the period consistently most in demand year after year.

## September: The Maturity Phase of the Season

September forms the second block of the season's mature weeks. The first ten days are still in the high-season band in terms of pricing; from the second ten days, prices drop by about 20-30%. In the third ten days, off-season pricing applies.

In September Bodrum's true quality is that the sea reaches its warmest state of the year. The water temperature is around 25-26 degrees, providing an ideal comfort for swimming. The air temperature is around 28-30 degrees; a milder period than the intense heat in the middle of the season. In the evenings, unlike the middle of the season, a slight coolness begins; dining on the villa terrace is comfortable.

Another distinctive quality of September is that the coves begin to empty. As the summer school season ends, the crowds arriving with families thin out. Coves that are busy in the middle of the season — such as Türkbükü, Göltürkbükü and Yalıkavak — show a marked calming from the second half of September.

## Early October: The Closing Hours of the Season

The first two weeks of October form the closing phase of the season. The air temperature is around 24-27 degrees; the sea is still 22-24 degrees and swimming is comfortable. The marina restaurants are still open, but the menus begin to simplify for the season's final weeks. A significant part of the beach club line continues to operate during these weeks.

After mid-October the weather begins to change; the first rains may be seen at the end of October. For guests staying in this period, a villa with a heated pool directly affects the comfort of the stay.

## Advantages: Price, Cove Calm, Ease of Reservation

An off-season stay has three fundamental advantages. The first is price. High-season (July-August) prices fall by 30-50% off-season. A villa that costs in the band of 4,500 TL/night in Türkbükü in high season drops to the band of 3,000 TL/night in the second half of September; this sets a difference of roughly 10,000 TL for a week.

The second is cove calm. Coves truly emptying is a quality that forms the essence of a boutique villa stay. In the middle of the season, even while a villa's pool is in use, external stimuli such as music stretching along the cove, the sound of a helicopter at the marina or a yacht passing seep into the atmosphere of the stay. Off-season these stimuli decrease markedly; the villa truly turns into an oasis.

The third is ease of reservation. Restaurant and beach club reservations that must be made three weeks in advance in the middle of the season can mostly be made the same day or a day in advance off-season. This keeps the stay's programme flexible; it adapts to plans that change during the day.

## Which Area for Which Week?

The ideal areas for May are Gümüşlük, Yalıkavak and Bitez. The cove of Gümüşlük reaches its purest state in May's light; the seafront restaurants have just opened with their weekly rhythm. Yalıkavak's marina is not crowded; the villas' hillside positions benefit from the season's serene atmosphere.

The ideal areas for September are Türkbükü, Göltürkbükü and Yalıkavak. Guests wary of the heavy crowds of these coves in the middle of the season can experience the calmer state of beach club culture from the second half of September. Macakızı and other addresses are still open, the music curation is still lively, but the rhythm is slower.

The ideal areas for October are Yalıkavak and Torba. Yalıkavak's marina stays lively even in the season's final weeks; Torba's calm northern cove suits the mature close of the season.

## Concierge Support and Off-Season Planning

An off-season stay requires a different approach in terms of concierge support compared with the middle of the season. The menu of some restaurants may have changed, some beach club addresses may have just closed the season. Our concierge team conveys in advance, according to your accommodation dates, which addresses are open and which kitchen line is lively.

A stay planned in the off-season weeks usually passes more smoothly than one planned in the middle of the season. There is no traffic, transfers are shorter, reservations are easily made. The true quality of the stay — the silence of the atmosphere — is experienced in its purest form during these weeks.
